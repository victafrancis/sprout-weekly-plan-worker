# 🌱 Weekly Developmental Plan  
**Age:** 10 months  
**Primary Focus Schema:** In-and-Out / Enveloping (Container play, hiding/revealing)  
**Secondary Focus:** Connecting / Disconnecting (Pulling apart Velcro, separating Legos, tearing)  
**Routine Focus:** Diapering & Feeding  

---

# 🎯 Weekly Goals (Observable)

**Goal 1 – Fine Motor & Cognitive (Schema)**  
Places objects into a container and removes them independently during play in at least 2 different contexts on 4 days this week.

**Goal 2 – Gross Motor (Mobility)**  
Cruises along furniture or takes 2–3 independent steps to retrieve a desired object or toy on at least 3 days this week.

**Goal 3 – Communication & Joint Attention**  
Points to an object or uses a specific gesture (like reaching) while making eye contact with a caregiver to request an item or action at least once per day.

---

# 🧺 Weekly Activity Menu (Pick 3–5 per day, repeat favorites)

Short (2–10 minute), flexible activity options.  
Repetition is encouraged.

---

## Monday
**1. Tumbler Drop (In-and-Out / Trajectory)**  
- Materials: Yumi’s favorite safe household tumblers and 3-4 large, soft items (e.g., rolled socks, large blocks).  
- Adult prompt: "Ready... drop!" Pause and wait for her to drop the item in, then let her dump it out.  
- Extend/Progress option: Move the tumbler a few steps away so she has to cruise or walk to drop the item.  

**2. Velcro Food Pull-Apart (Connecting / Disconnecting)**  
- Materials: Velcro play food pieces.  
- Adult prompt: Hand her a connected piece. "Can you pull it apart?" Model the action if needed, emphasizing the "crrrck" sound.  
- Extend/Progress option: Hide the separated pieces under a small cloth for her to find (Enveloping).  

**3. Mirror Dancing (Gross Motor / Orientation)**  
- Materials: A floor mirror or low wall mirror, favorite music.  
- Adult prompt: Stand or sit next to her. "Look, there's Yumi!" Encourage her to pull to stand and bounce or step to the music while watching her reflection.  

---

## Tuesday
**1. Peek-a-Boo Box (Enveloping / Object Permanence)**  
- Materials: A small cardboard box or lid, and a favorite toy.  
- Adult prompt: Hide the toy under the box. "Where did it go?" Wait for her to lift the box to reveal the toy.  
- Extend option: Let her hide the toy and you act surprised when you find it.  

**2. Cruising for Legos (Gross Motor / Transporting)**  
- Materials: Large Lego blocks placed spaced out along the edge of the couch.  
- Adult prompt: "Can you get the red block?" Encourage her to cruise along the furniture to collect them.  

**3. Tissue Box Pull (In-and-Out / Disconnecting)**  
- Context: Floor play.  
- Prompt: Stuff a clean, empty tissue box with scraps of fabric or safe, large pieces of tissue paper. Let her pull them out one by one using her pincer grasp.  

---

## Wednesday
**1. Shallow Water Transfer (In-and-Out / Sensory)**  
- Materials: A baking sheet or shallow bin with a tiny amount of water, and a few small plastic cups.  
- Adult prompt: Show her how to scoop and pour. "Splash! The water goes in." Let her explore filling and emptying the cups. *(Requires constant, arm's-reach supervision).*  

**2. Sensory Book Pointing (Communication)**  
- Materials: Interactive or sensory board books.  
- Adult prompt: Point to a texture or flap. "Look at the soft bunny." Pause and wait for her to point or touch it with her index finger.  

**3. Feeding Routine Integration**  
- Script:  
  “It's time to eat. I am putting your bowl here.” (pause)  
  “Do you want the banana or the strawberry?” (hold up both, wait for her to point or reach)  
  “You picked the banana. You are using your fingers to eat.”  

---

## Thursday
**1. Lego Separation Station (Connecting / Disconnecting)**  
- Materials: 4-5 large Lego blocks, loosely connected in pairs.  
- Adult prompt: "Pull!" Encourage her to use her hands and wrists to separate the blocks.  

**2. Humidifier Mist Tracking (Visual Tracking / Trajectory)**  
- Materials: The household humidifier (safely out of reach but visible).  
- Adult prompt: Sit together and watch the mist. "Up, up, up." Drop a light play scarf nearby to show how it falls compared to the mist rising.  

**3. Choice-Based Play (Communication)**  
- Prompt strategy: Hold up two distinct toys (e.g., a block and a Velcro food item). Ask, "Which one?" Wait for her to point, reach, or make eye contact before handing it to her.  

---

## Friday
**1. Sensory Bin Dig (Enveloping / Pincer Grasp)**  
- Materials: A shallow bin filled with large, safe items (e.g., large rolled oats or crumpled paper) with a few toys hidden inside.  
- Adult prompt: "Where is the block? Let's dig!" Encourage her to use her hands to uncover the items.  

**2. Lid and Box Puzzle (Rotational / Connecting)**  
- Materials: 2-3 small boxes or Tupperware containers with easy-to-remove lids.  
- Adult prompt: "Can you take the lid off?" Let her practice pulling the lids off and attempting to put them back on.  

**3. Choice-Based Interaction**  
- Offer: “Do you want to read a book or play with water?”  
- Pause for response (look for pointing, reaching, or vocalizing).  

---

## Weekend (Repeat & Extend)

Repeat high-engagement activities from the week (e.g., Water Transfer, Velcro Pull-Apart).  
Optional progression ideas:
- **Combine Schemas:** Give her a small container to hold while she cruises along the couch, allowing her to drop Legos into the container as she moves (Transporting + In-and-Out + Gross Motor).  
- **Paper Tearing:** Supervise closely while she tears junk mail or tissue paper, practicing her pincer grasp and wrist rotation, then have her drop the pieces into a tumbler.  
- **Standing Play:** Move the Lid and Box puzzle to a low coffee table so she has to stand independently or cruise to engage with it.

# 👶 Caregiving-as-Learning Scripts

### Diapering
- “I’m going to change your diaper now.” (pause, wait for eye contact)  
- “Can you help by lifting your legs up?” (wait for her to lift or shift her weight)  
- Narrate actions slowly: "I am wiping. Now the clean diaper goes on."  
- Offer micro-choice: "Do you want to hold the clean diaper or this block while I change you?"  
*(Note: If she is very active, you can practice standing diaper changes while she holds onto a safe surface).*

### Feeding
- Describe child’s action: "You picked up the Velcro strawberry with your fingers."  
- Pause for response: Wait for her to look at you, babble, or smile.  
- Offer simple choices: "Water or milk?" (Hold up both cups).  
- Encourage independence: Pre-load a spoon and lay it on her tray, allowing her to pick it up and bring it to her mouth herself.  

---

# 🔎 What to Observe This Week

Log brief notes (1–2 minutes daily):

- **Mobility:** How long is she standing alone? Is she taking more independent steps between furniture?
- **Fine Motor:** Is she successfully getting objects *into* the tumblers/containers, or mostly dumping them *out*?
- **Communication:** Is she pointing with her index finger to request things, or mostly reaching? Is she making eye contact when she babbles "mama"?
- **Schema repetition pattern:** Does she prefer pulling things apart (Velcro/Legos) or putting things in containers?
- **Participation in routines:** Is she anticipating the steps of diapering or feeding?

---

# 🔁 End-of-Week Reflection

Ask:

- Which activities held attention longest? (e.g., Water play vs. Legos)
- Did frustration occur? (If yes → simplify next week. For example, if Legos are too hard to pull apart, loosen them first).
- Is repetition increasing skill? (Is she getting faster at dropping items into the tumblers?)
- Is engagement becoming more intentional? (Is she looking at you to share her excitement when she finds a hidden toy?)
- Any new dominant schema emerging? (e.g., Is she starting to carry items around the room more often? -> Transporting).

---

# ➡️ How Next Week Might Progress

If primary schema (In-and-Out/Enveloping) is strong:
- Add complexity by using containers with smaller openings (like a clean, empty wipes dispenser) to challenge her pincer grasp.
- Introduce wrapping toys in play silks for her to unwrap.

If secondary skills (Connecting/Disconnecting) are emerging:
- Introduce large pop-tubes or Mega Bloks.
- Combine schemas by having her pull apart Velcro food and then sort the pieces into two different containers.

If frustration appears:
- Simplify materials (use boxes with no lids instead of tight lids).  
- Increase object size (larger blocks are easier to grasp).  
- Shorten duration of focused activities and follow her lead back to gross motor play.  

---

# 🛟 Safety Reminders
- **Choking Risk:** Remove all small objects. Ensure paper tearing activities are strictly supervised so paper does not go in her mouth. Velcro food pieces and Legos must be large enough to pass a choke-tube test.
- **Water Safety:** Supervise water play constantly and within arm’s reach. Empty all bins immediately after use.
- **Mobility Safety:** Ensure all furniture she uses for cruising/pulling to stand is heavy and anchored to the wall.
- **Cords/Strings:** Keep humidifier cords and blind cords completely out of reach.
