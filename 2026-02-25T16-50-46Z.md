# 🌱 Weekly Developmental Plan  
**Age:** 10 months  
**Primary Focus Schema:** In-and-Out & Enveloping (Container play, hiding/revealing)  
**Secondary Focus:** Connecting (Velcro, interlocking blocks) & Trajectory (Dropping)  
**Routine Focus:** Diapering & Feeding  

**Child Information:**
- **Age (in months):** 10 months  
- **Mobility level:** Crawls confidently, cruises holding furniture, stands alone briefly (10 seconds), taking a few independent steps.  
- **Communication level:** Babbles ("mamama"), says "mama", points with index finger, uses specific gestures (reaching), initiates joint attention.  
- **Current interests / repeated behaviors observed:** Container play (filling/emptying), dropping/throwing, peek-a-boo, water play, Velcro play food, Lego blocks, paper tearing/pulling, household objects (tumblers).  
- **Any sensitivities or frustrations:** Testing boundaries / social referencing (typical for age).  
- **Environment notes:** Home space, access to safe household objects.  
- **Parent priority focus:** Supporting emerging independent walking and encouraging back-and-forth communication.  

---

# 🎯 Weekly Goals (Observable)

**Goal 1 – Fine Motor & Cognitive (Schema)**  
Places objects into a container and removes them independently in at least 2 different play contexts on 4 days this week.

**Goal 2 – Gross Motor**  
Transitions from pulling to stand at furniture to taking 1–2 independent steps to reach a desired object or caregiver on 3 days this week.

**Goal 3 – Communication & Joint Attention**  
Uses a specific gesture (e.g., pointing or reaching) combined with eye contact to request an object or initiate an interaction at least 2 times per day.

---

# 🧺 Weekly Activity Menu (Pick 3–5 per day, repeat favorites)

Short (2–10 minute), flexible activity options.  
Repetition is encouraged.

---

## Monday
**1. Tumbler Drop (In-and-Out / Trajectory)**  
- Materials: 2–3 safe household tumblers (plastic or metal) and large, soft items (e.g., rolled socks, large blocks).  
- Adult prompt: "In it goes!" Drop an item into the tumbler, then hand one to Yumi. Wait for her to try.  
- Extend/Progress option: Have her stand at a low table while dropping items to practice standing balance.  

**2. Velcro Food Pull (Connecting)**  
- Materials: Velcro play food.  
- Adult prompt: Hold one side of the food while she pulls the other. Say, "Pull!" as it separates.  
- Extend/Progress option: Let her try to push the Velcro pieces back together (Connecting).  

**3. Peek-a-Boo Towel (Enveloping)**  
- Materials: A small towel or cloth.  
- Adult prompt: Hide a favorite toy under the towel. Ask, "Where did it go?" Wait for her to pull the towel away.  

---

## Tuesday
**1. Cruising for Blocks (Gross Motor / Transporting)**  
- Materials: Lego blocks or Velcro food pieces.  
- Adult prompt: Place the toys spaced out along the couch. Encourage Yumi to cruise from one end to the other to collect them.  
- Extend option: Stand slightly away from the couch with a toy to encourage 1–2 independent steps toward you.  

**2. Tissue Box Pull (In-and-Out / Enveloping)**  
- Materials: An empty tissue box stuffed with safe fabric scraps or play scarves.  
- Adult prompt: Pull the first scarf out halfway. "Look! Can you pull?"  

**3. Routine Integration (Diapering)**  
- Context: During a diaper change.  
- Prompt: Hand Yumi a clean diaper to hold. "Can you hold your diaper?" This gives her a "job" and utilizes her grasping skills.  

---

## Wednesday
**1. Water Pour & Splash (Trajectory / Water Play)**  
- Materials: A shallow baking dish with a small amount of water, a plastic tumbler, and a sponge.  
- Adult prompt: Show her how to squeeze the sponge or pour water from the tumbler. "Splash!"  

**2. Paper Tearing Station (Fine Motor)**  
- Materials: Junk mail, tissue paper, or old magazines.  
- Adult prompt: Make a small rip in the paper and hand it to her. "Riiiip." (Supervise closely to ensure no paper goes in the mouth).  

**3. Routine Focus Activity (Feeding)**  
- Script:  
  "Here is your food." (pause)  
  "You are picking up the banana." (pause)  
  Wait for her to look at you, then smile and nod. "Yum."  

---

## Thursday
**1. Mirror Play & Pointing (Communication / Orientation)**  
- Materials: A floor mirror or low wall mirror.  
- Adult prompt: Sit next to her in front of the mirror. Point to her reflection. "There's Yumi!" Wait to see if she points back or touches the mirror.  

**2. Standing Drop (Trajectory / Gross Motor)**  
- Materials: A laundry basket and several large toys.  
- Adult prompt: Have Yumi stand holding onto the basket. Hand her a toy to drop in. "Ready, set, drop!"  

**3. Communication or Social Activity**  
- Prompt strategy: Place a favorite toy (like a Lego block) just out of reach. Wait for her to point or reach for it. As soon as she makes eye contact and gestures, hand it to her immediately: "You want the block! Here it is."  

---

## Friday
**1. Sensory Bin Dig (Enveloping)**  
- Materials: A large container filled with safe, large items (e.g., big rolled socks, large balls) with a few smaller favorite toys hidden at the bottom.  
- Adult prompt: "Where is the block?" Let her dig through the items to find the hidden toy.  

**2. Lego Pull-Apart (Connecting)**  
- Materials: Large interlocking Lego blocks.  
- Adult prompt: Connect two blocks loosely. Hand them to her and pause. Let her use her pincer grasp and wrist rotation to pull them apart.  

**3. Choice-Based Interaction**  
- Offer: Hold up two items (e.g., a tumbler and a Velcro apple). "Tumbler or apple?"  
- Pause for response: Wait for her to point, reach, or vocalize her choice before handing it to her.  

---

## Weekend (Repeat & Extend)

Repeat high-engagement activities (like Water Play or Velcro Pull).  
Optional progression ideas:
- [Progression 1] Move the laundry basket slightly further away during the "Standing Drop" to encourage her to take a step to reach it.  
- [Progression 2] Add lids to the tumblers or containers. Let her practice taking the lids off before putting the objects inside (Rotational + In-and-Out).  
- [Progression 3] Hide yourself behind a door or couch for a gross-motor game of peek-a-boo, encouraging her to crawl or walk to find you.

# 👶 Caregiving-as-Learning Scripts

### Diapering
- "I’m going to change your diaper now." (pause for eye contact)  
- "Can you help by lifting your legs?" (wait for her to move or still her body)  
- Narrate actions slowly: "I am taking off the Velcro tabs. Pull!"  
- Offer micro-choice if appropriate: "Do you want to hold the wipes or the clean diaper?"  

### Feeding
- Describe child’s action: "You are holding the spoon."  
- Pause for response: Wait for her to look at you or babble.  
- Offer simple choices: Hold up two safe finger foods. "Do you want the berry or the cracker?"  
- Encourage independence: Pre-load a spoon and lay it on her tray, allowing her to pick it up and bring it to her mouth using her developing wrist rotation.

# 🔎 What to Observe This Week

Log brief notes (1–2 minutes daily):

- **Emerging skill:** Is she taking more independent steps when moving between furniture?  
- **Schema repetition pattern:** Does she prefer putting things *into* containers, or taking them *out*?  
- **Communication attempt:** Is she making eye contact when pointing or reaching for an object?  
- **Fine motor:** Is she using her index finger and thumb (pincer grasp) to pull apart the Velcro or Legos?  
- **Participation in routines:** Is she anticipating the steps of diapering (e.g., lifting legs)?  

---

# 🔁 End-of-Week Reflection

Ask:

- Which activities held attention longest? (e.g., Water play vs. Velcro)  
- Did frustration occur? (If yes → simplify next week by loosening the Legos or using wider containers).  
- Is repetition increasing skill? (Is she getting faster at pulling the Velcro apart?)  
- Is engagement becoming more intentional? (Is she looking at you to share her excitement?)  
- Any new dominant schema emerging? (e.g., Is she starting to spin objects or focus heavily on lids?)  

---

# ➡️ How Next Week Might Progress

If primary schema (In-and-Out / Enveloping) is strong:
- Add complexity: Introduce containers with smaller openings to challenge her pincer grasp and wrist rotation.  
- Add variation: Introduce "posting" activities (dropping large coins or playing cards into a slot cut in a box).  

If secondary skills (Walking / Connecting) are emerging:
- Extension idea: Encourage her to carry a lightweight object (like a plastic tumbler) while taking independent steps (Transporting + Gross Motor).  
- Combine two schemas: Have her pull apart Velcro food (Connecting) and drop the pieces into a bucket (Trajectory/In-and-Out).  

If frustration appears:
- Simplify materials: Use larger blocks that are easier to pull apart.  
- Reduce distance: Move toys closer during cruising so she doesn't have to stretch as far.  
- Shorten duration: Keep activities to 2–3 minutes.  

---

# 🛟 Safety Reminders
- **Choking Risk:** Ensure all Legos, Velcro food pieces, and items used for container play are large enough that they cannot fit inside a toilet paper tube. 
- **Water Safety:** Supervise water play constantly and within arm’s reach. Empty all water bins immediately after use.
- **Paper Tearing:** Supervise closely during paper tearing to ensure Yumi does not put paper in her mouth (oral exploration is still high).
- **Climbing/Cruising:** Ensure furniture used for cruising is heavy and stable (won't tip over if she pulls up on it). Clear the floor of tripping hazards.
