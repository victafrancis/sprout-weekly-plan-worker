# 🌱 Weekly Developmental Plan

**Age:** 10 Months
**Primary Schemas:** Trajectory (dropping/throwing) & Enveloping (hiding/revealing, in/out)
**Key Interests:** Water play, Books, Music, Sensory bins, Mirrors, Oral exploration
**Routine Focus:** Mealtime (Communication & Practical Life) & Play Transitions

## 📊 Logic for this Week's Plan
*Based on the "Decision Rules" for home planning:*
* **Gross Motor Progression:** Since your baby is pulling to stand, cruising, and taking independent steps, we will elevate trajectory activities from floor-level to standing-level to encourage balance and endurance. 
* **Fine Motor & Cognitive Integration:** Your baby has mastered the pincer grasp and object permanence. We will upgrade enveloping activities to include smaller, safer targets and multi-layered hiding games that require precise finger movements. 
* **Communication Focus:** With joint attention, pointing, and specific words ("mama") emerging, activities will specifically script "tarry time" (waiting) to prompt their index finger pointing and vocal requests.

## 🎯 Weekly Goals (Observable)

**Goal 1 – Gross Motor & Trajectory**
Drops or throws an object into a target while maintaining a standing position (holding on with one hand or standing alone) at least 3 times per day.

**Goal 2 – Fine Motor & Enveloping**
Uses a pincer grasp to successfully extract a partially hidden object from a sensory bin or container across 3 different play sessions.

**Goal 3 – Joint Attention & Communication**
Uses index finger to point at a desired object or book, while making eye contact with the caregiver, before the caregiver provides the item.

## 🧺 Weekly Activity Menu

*Pick 3–5 per day. Focus on repetition, letting your baby guide the duration.*

### Monday

**1. Brown Rice Sensory Dig (Enveloping + Pincer Grasp)**
* **Materials:** A shallow baking dish filled with dry brown rice and a few small (but strictly non-choking hazard) toys buried inside.
* **Action:** Encourage your baby to dig in the brown rice to uncover the toys, using their pincer grasp to pick out the items.
* **Why it helps:** Combines the enveloping schema (hiding/uncovering) with fine motor practice. Dry brown rice provides excellent tactile feedback for sensory and oral explorers while remaining a safe, food-grade household item.

**2. Standing Target Practice (Trajectory + Gross Motor)**
* **Materials:** Laundry basket, soft balls, a sturdy couch.
* **Action:** Place the basket next to the couch. Have the baby pull to stand, then hand them a ball to drop into the basket.
* **Why it helps:** Integrates their active trajectory schema with cruising/standing milestones. It forces them to weight-bear on one hand or stand independently for a few seconds while dropping the item.

**3. "Mama" Mirror Reveal (Object Permanence + Mirrors)**
* **Action:** Sit in front of a mirror with a towel. Cover your reflection, ask "Where's Mama?", and let the baby pull the towel down.
* **Why it helps:** Capitalizes on their understanding of object permanence and interest in mirrors, reinforcing their newly acquired word "mama" in a highly engaging, back-and-forth social game. 

### Tuesday

**1. Water Flow Station (Trajectory + Active Water Play)**
* **Materials:** Shallow baking pan with a tiny amount of water, measuring cups, and a sponge.
* **Action:** Let the baby squeeze the sponge or pour water from the cup into the pan (strictly supervised).
* **Why it helps:** Satisfies their interest in uncontained water play and the continuous "flow" aspect of the trajectory schema, while letting them practice their grasp on the sponge.

**2. Point & Read (Communication + Books)**
* **Action:** Hold an interactive/sensory book just out of reach. Wait for the baby to point with their index finger or babble before bringing it closer to read.
* **Why it helps:** Uses the RIE/Montessori concept of "serve-and-return" to map their physical gestures (pointing) to a highly desired reward (the book), strengthening intentional communication.

**3. High Chair Wipe-Down (Routine Focus - Practical Life)**
* **Action:** After a meal, hand your baby a small cloth dampened *only* with water. Let them practice wiping their high chair tray.
* **Why it helps:** Montessori practical life treats care-of-environment as formative education for independence. Using a plain water-dampened cloth keeps it completely chemical-free and safe for oral explorers, while they practice broad arm movements.

### Wednesday

**1. The "Post Box" (Enveloping + Pincer Grasp)**
* **Materials:** A shoe box with a narrow slot cut in the top, and several jar lids.
* **Action:** The baby uses their pincer grasp to pick up a lid and manipulate it to fit into the slot.
* **Why it helps:** This is an advanced enveloping and container play task that requires wrist rotation and fine motor control, tailored to their 10-month dexterity.

**2. Dancing Drops (Trajectory + Music)**
* **Action:** Play their favorite music. Hold them up or let them cruise while handing them objects (like sensory scarves or beanbags) to throw to the beat.
* **Why it helps:** Melds their interest in music and dancing with the trajectory schema, making gross motor exertion feel like a highly rewarding game. 

**3. Layered Peek-a-Boo (Object Permanence)**
* **Action:** Hide a musical toy under a box, and then put a blanket over the box. 
* **Why it helps:** Because your baby already understands basic object permanence, adding a second layer (blanket *then* box) challenges their cognitive sequencing and memory. 

### Thursday

**1. Bath Time Splash & Drop (Trajectory + Water Play)**
* **Action:** During a supervised bath, give them heavy, sealed plastic bottles to drop into the water from a seated position to create big splashes.
* **Why it helps:** Explores cause-and-effect and the physics of water displacement, leaning heavily into their trajectory schema.

**2. Sensory Bottle Shakers (Container Play + Music)**
* **Materials:** Empty, clean plastic bottles filled securely with dry beans or rice. 
* **Action:** Let the baby shake them to the music, drop them, and watch the contents settle.
* **Why it helps:** Acts as a homemade instrument, linking container play, enveloping (things inside a clear shell), and music.

**3. The Reaching Game (Communication)**
* **Action:** Place a highly desired toy on a low table where they must pull to stand to see it. Wait for them to use their specific reaching gesture. 
* **Why it helps:** Creates a natural environment opportunity to practice standing independently while using their newly formed communication gestures.

### Friday

**1. Pincer Grasp Water Rescue (Fine Motor + Water Play)**
* **Materials:** A shallow tray with a little water and some floating, baby-safe objects (like large rings or rubber ducks).
* **Action:** Have the baby use their index finger and thumb to pluck the slippery items out of the water.
* **Why it helps:** The resistance and slipperiness of the water make using the pincer grasp more challenging and rewarding.

**2. Scarf Pull Box (Enveloping)**
* **Materials:** An empty tissue box stuffed with sensory scarves or scrap fabric.
* **Action:** The baby pulls the scarves out one by one, then tries to stuff them back in.
* **Why it helps:** Deeply satisfies the enveloping schema (emptying and filling containers) and allows safe oral exploration of the soft fabrics.

**3. Follow the Leader Crawl (Gross Motor)**
* **Action:** Get on the floor and crawl confidently to another room, looking back to initiate joint attention and encourage them to follow.
* **Why it helps:** Reinforces their confident crawling while practicing social engagement and back-and-forth interaction.

### Weekend (Repeat & Extend)

* Repeat the brown rice bin or water flow station, as repetition is foundational to schema learning.
* If they are throwing items with high accuracy, provide a smaller target basket to progress the difficulty. 

## 👶 Caregiving-as-Learning Scripts

**Mealtime (Autonomy & Communication)**
* *Setup:* Offer finger foods to encourage that pincer grasp.
* *Script:* "Do you want more?" (Wait for the point or 'mamama' vocalization). "You pointed to the strawberry. Here is the strawberry."
* *Why:* Contingent "serve-and-return" interactions during routines heavily support language acquisition.

**Play Transitions (Joint Attention)**
* *Setup:* When moving from one activity to the next.
* *Script:* Get down on their level. "We are all done with the water." (Wait for eye contact). "Let's go read a book." 
* *Why:* Respectful transitions build trust and practice sustained joint attention.

## 🔎 What to Observe This Week

* **Mobility:** How long can they stand alone before dropping back down? Are they attempting to take steps without holding onto furniture?
* **Communication:** Are they combining the point *with* eye contact, or just pointing at the object? 
* **Schemas:** When they drop an object, do they look to see where it landed? (Indicates cognitive mapping of the trajectory).

## 🔁 End-of-Week Decision Rules

* **Continue:** If the "Post Box" is frustrating, switch to a wider bucket but keep the same general activity.
* **Progress:** If they are standing alone easily, try handing them objects that require *two* hands to hold/drop, forcing them to balance without holding the couch.
* **Pivot:** If they start obsessively putting things into bags and carrying them around, we will shift focus to the **Transporting** schema next week.
